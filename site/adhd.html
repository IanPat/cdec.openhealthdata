ADHD

This site takes data from sources like the Health and Social Care Information Centre (HSCIC) and shows how it can be turned into visualisations and analysis to answer specific questions for your organisation. This is the ALPHA version of the site

This page is an exploration of ADHD prescriptions across the UK

(Uncleaned) ADHD prescription variance across the UK

This chart shows how many prescriptions are issued across the UK per registered patient in that area.

This is the original raw data, aggregated per CCG then normalised by patient count

As can be seen, there are some clear outliers in the data (with 8x standard deviation and 4x standard deviation in two cases!)

ADHD Prescription Distribution

To better understand the distribution of this data, we can map this variance into a histogram

It is clear from this initial histogram that the outliers are so far off the scale to be skewing the whole result set

This data can be removed and the process repeated

ADHD prescription variance across the UK

This chart shows how many prescriptions are issued across the UK per registered patient in that area.

This is the filtered data, aggregated per CCG then normalised by patient count, with the obvious outliers having been pre-removed

ADHD Prescription Distribution

Mapping the distribution of the cleaned data to a histogram, we can see that the data is still skewed to the low side of the mean

This looks like a "log normal distribution", and is most likely the result of having combined several datasets with a multiplicative effect

CCG Drilldown

There are some interesting outliers in the map of England, we can dive down into the CCGs to investigate whether this is the result of overall more prescriptions per GP being handed out, or there being a few specialists in the CCG
